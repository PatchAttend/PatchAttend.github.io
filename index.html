Attend to the Right Patches for Temporally Consistent Video Editing

Paper(Under review) arXiv(Coming Soon) Code(Coming Soon)

Abstract

Prior research using text-to-image (TTI) diffusion models has effectively edited videos to match text descriptions while preserving the visual setup with minimal computing. Yet, these methods struggle with videos that have significant motion or camera angle changes. They often fail to capture the original video's motion accurately or to restore detailed background scenes.

Our paper introduces PatchAttend, a TTI-based framework that edits videos in a one-shot tuning manner, guided by text for real-world videos. PatchAttend maintains motion detail from the source by linking each video frame's 2D pixels to their corresponding 3D points. We use novel attention mechanisms that factor in tracking results to enhance temporal coherence. Our tuning techniques preserve the ability of pre-trained models and avoid the problem of overfitting. We perform cross-attention manipulation for precise and effective editing.

Our comprehensive experiments show the superiority of PatchAttend in temporal consistency, editing accuracy, and in restoring unedited parts of the video. We will release our code soon.
